Your `colvars.conf` and `in.lammps` files look well-structured for defining collective variables and using them in a LAMMPS simulation with the colvars module. Here's a brief overview and a few tips to ensure everything works smoothly:

### colvars.conf
- **tolerance_factor**: Measures the distance between two groups of atoms (1-10 and 11-20).
- **octahedral_tilt**: Measures an angle defined by three groups of atoms.
- **oxidation_state_variance**: Uses a custom function to compute the variance of the oxidation states for atoms 1, 2, 11, and 12.

Make sure:
- The `oxidation_state` property is defined and accessible in your LAMMPS simulation (e.g., as an atom property or via a user-defined compute).
- The atom indices correspond correctly to your system.

### in.lammps
- You correctly load the colvars module with `fix mycv all colvars outputs/colvars.conf`.
- Ensure the `outputs/` directory exists or LAMMPS will fail to write the output files.
- Add any necessary force field, neighbor, timestep, and run commands after the fix.
